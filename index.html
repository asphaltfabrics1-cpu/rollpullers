Why crews prefer RollPullers layouts.

Adaptive by design

Telescoping arms on bearings, crab-claw swing arms, and a patented middle arm let crews jump between centered runs and curb-hugging passes without re-rigging.

  • Short-roll installs from 1–10 ft
  • Brush extensions to sweep ~18 ft
  • Oil-truck mounting for one-pass oil + fabric

Consistent laydown quality

Dual-bar PVC tension plus optional roller systems stretch fabrics before they hit the brush so wrinkles disappear and seams line up tight.

  • Laser-cut tension plates with 0°–45° pinning
  • Polypropylene extra-fill brushes rated to ~325°F
  • Rotating spindle roll holders with tunable brakes

Innovation timeline

1980s · Patented telescoping laydown concept

Transition from bolt-together frames to telescoping systems that hug the fabric to the road.

2000s · Global deployment

Universal mounts and crab-claw swing arms enter the lineup, keeping installs tight to obstacles.

2016 · Roll Puller™ redesign

Deeper core grip, laser-cut teeth, and forged shafts make container unloading a single-operator job.

2019–2024 · Upgrade kits

Articulating center brush mounts, roller tension systems, and heavier spindle assemblies retrofit legacy fleets.
