Patented systems built into every Grizzly.

Super-heavy double-bar frame

A rigid dual-bar backbone provides four times the torsional strength of early bolt-together rigs. Integrated brackets accept universal mounts, brush/PVC extensions, and the short-roll arm without custom welding.

Telescoping dual arms on bearings

Each arm slides on replaceable bearings for smooth extension, keeping brush ends exactly flush with the fabric edge. Add the hydraulic telescope kit for push-button width changes.

Articulating brush system

Two outside jacks set brush angle, while a center mount articulates to match the road crown. Optional brush extensions and polypropylene “extra-fill” brushes (≈50% more filament) sweep seamlessly up to 18 ft.

Dual-bar PVC tension + roller options

Six heavy-duty sliding brackets double-tension the fabric before it reaches the brush. Add the dual-PVC roller system for grids or hybrid mats that need extra stretch.

Rotating spindle roll holders

Disc brakes govern roll speed and adjust instantly for curves. Wear parts such as pads, T-screws, and nuts stay inexpensive and field-serviceable.

Solid steel cones for 3–5 in cores

Machined cones seat firmly inside typical cardboard cores and can be sharpened, ensuring secure grip even on rough roads.

Crab-claw swing arms

Dual mechanical arms (single on Cub, optional hydraulic) let crews lay fabric within six inches of curbs, guardrails, or parapet walls while keeping the tractor safely offset.

Oil-truck mounting frame

A rear-mounted frame lifts the Grizzly hydraulically so one vehicle can lay oil and fabric simultaneously, then drops back onto a tractor in minutes.

Upgrade paths for legacy fleets (2019–2024 kits).

Brush & tension upgrades

Retrofit a four-position articulating center brush mount, add laser-cut plates that pin the multi-bar tension system from 0° to 45°, and integrate the roller tension module for grid or composite materials.

Structural improvements

Updated telescoping tubes include fresh bearings and seals, while rotating spindle roll holders now use heavier shafts and redesigned brake hardware for longer service intervals.
